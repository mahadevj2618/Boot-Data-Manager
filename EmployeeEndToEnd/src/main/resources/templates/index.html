Employee

CRUD operations in employee management encompass the fundamental tasks of Create, Read, Update, and Delete. Creating involves adding new employee records to the system, capturing essential details such as name, contact information, position, and salary. Reading allows users to retrieve employee information, enabling searches based on criteria like employee ID or name to view their details. Updating entails modifying existing records, allowing changes to be made to fields such as salary, position, or contact information. Lastly, deleting removes employee records from the database, typically based on an identifier, ensuring the database remains current and accurate. Together, these operations form the backbone of managing employee data efficiently and securely within any organization.
